Cookie Policy

Last updated: April 9, 2026

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work efficiently, provide a better user experience, and supply analytical information to website owners.

This Cookie Policy explains how the Raven Wallet website ("Website") uses cookies and similar tracking technologies.

2. How We Use Cookies

The Raven Wallet website uses minimal cookies to ensure the best possible experience. We categorize our cookies as follows:

2.1 Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the Website to function and cannot be disabled. They include:

Cookie Purpose Duration
session_id Maintains your session state Session
cookie_consent Stores your cookie consent preference 1 year
theme_pref Stores your display theme preference 1 year

2.2 Analytics Cookies

These cookies help us understand how visitors interact with our Website by collecting and reporting information anonymously.

Cookie Purpose Duration
_ga Google Analytics — Distinguishes unique visitors 2 years
_ga_* Google Analytics — Maintains session state 2 years

2.3 Functional Cookies

These cookies enable enhanced functionality and personalization:

Cookie Purpose Duration
locale Stores your language preference 1 year
download_ref Tracks which download link you used 30 days

3. Mobile Application

The Raven Wallet mobile application does not use browser cookies. The app may use local storage mechanisms (such as AsyncStorage or SecureStore) to store your preferences and session data. These are governed by our Privacy Policy.

4. Third-Party Cookies

Some third-party services we use may set their own cookies. These include:

  • Google Analytics: For website traffic analysis. Google's Privacy Policy
  • Google Play / Apple App Store: Download link referral tracking
  • Social Media Platforms: If you interact with embedded social media content

We do not control third-party cookies and recommend reviewing their respective policies.

5. Managing Cookies

You can control and manage cookies in several ways:

5.1 Browser Settings

Most browsers allow you to refuse or accept cookies, delete existing cookies, and set preferences for certain websites. Here's how to manage cookies in popular browsers:

  • Google Chrome
  • Mozilla Firefox
  • Apple Safari
  • Microsoft Edge

5.2 Opt-Out Tools

  • Google Analytics Opt-Out Browser Add-on
  • Your Online Choices (EU users)
Note: Disabling essential cookies may affect the functionality of our Website. Analytics cookies are optional and you can opt out without any impact on your browsing experience.

6. Do Not Track

Some browsers include a "Do Not Track" (DNT) feature. We currently honor DNT signals by disabling analytics cookies when DNT is detected.
